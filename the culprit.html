How to Play

Goal

Every round, a crime has been committed. The innocents must figure out who the Culprit(s) are — before they outnumber you!

Setup

  • Enter your crew name and how many players are playing (4–10).
  • Each player enters their name one by one.
  • Each player gets a secret card with their job. Only they see it.
  • Culprits know they're guilty. Innocents don't know who is guilty.

Number of Culprits

  • 4 players → 1 Culprit
  • 5–6 players → 2 Culprits
  • 7–9 players → 3 Culprits
  • 10 players → 4 Culprits

Gameplay

  • Introduce yourselves: Each player says their job role — but Culprits can lie about it!
  • The Crime: Find out what terrible thing happened.
  • Discussion: Talk and share your suspicions.
  • Clue: A clue is revealed after each vote round.
  • Vote: Everyone votes for who they suspect. Highest vote gets eliminated.

Winning

  • Innocents win when all Culprits are eliminated.
  • Culprits win when they equal or outnumber the innocents remaining.

Tiebreaker

If votes are tied, the group must agree on someone to eliminate (rock-paper-scissors, flip a coin, argue louder — dealer's choice).

Tips

  • Watch body language and inconsistencies in stories.
  • Culprits: stay calm, deflect suspicion, blend in.
  • Innocents: use clues wisely, trust your gut.
